Bus ETA — Mobile User Flow

Primary path (single thumb): open → search → arrivals → back. Alerts surface inline on the arrivals screen. Every control ≥ 44 px; content capped at 480 px.
Primary navigation edge Secondary / back edge Weather warning High-relevance incident Route/Stop chip
Design rationale. The app is a two-screen mobile flow. ① Search offers debounced server-backed autocomplete over routes and stops (with quick-pick chips for the four most common routes) and routes the user to either a route-scoped board or a stop board. ② Arrivals is the combined stop/route screen: a brand header, the live ETA list (pulsing “Due / N min” pills), and — when present — inline weather warnings and traffic incidents ranked by relevance (HIGH/MED/LOW). ③ Alerts are not a separate screen; they render in-place above/below the ETA list so a rider sees context without leaving the board. A thumb-reachable “Back to search” bar is pinned to the bottom of every results view for single-handed use. Language (EN/繁/简) is global via the top bar. Empty, error, and offline/demo states share the same reserved-height slots to keep CLS at 0.
